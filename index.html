Diabetes in the US from 2000 to 2021

Diabetes has been a critical health issue that has impacted society throughout the years. One of the most pressing concerns facing the United States over the past two decades has been the rise in diabetes cases. From 2000 to 2021, this once-considered "silent epidemic" has ballooned into a significant public health challenge, affecting millions of individuals across the nation.

Diabetes Prevalence Trends by Age Group

    The younger age groups do not suffer as much from Type-2 Diabetes, that attributes to the lower percentages
    and the stagnant growth rate. It is also apparent that the older age groups are more prone to diabetes, which
    is to be expected. However, the growing trend in those older age groups should be a cause for concern.

    This could be attributed to many reasons, but it would likely be poor eating habits and
    could also be linked to the growing trend of obesity in America. The recent downward trend
    could be a good sign, however, it could also be due to recent advancements in technology.
    Further data and cross referencing would be necessary.

Proportion of Diabetics in the US

    Click on any of the states to see that states' proportion of diabetics over the years.

    It is clear throughout the years that the southern and the southeastern parts of the US has suffered more from diabetes
    as a whole compared to the rest of the US. In the early 2000's many of the States were relatively similar in proportion
    of diabetics, however, the first signs of increase starts in the southeast and that trend continues throughout the years.

States with the Highest Proportion of Diabetics
